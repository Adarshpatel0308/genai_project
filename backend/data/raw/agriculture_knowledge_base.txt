## WHEAT CULTIVATION GUIDE

### Varieties
- HD 2967: High yielding, rust resistant, suitable for North India
- PBW 343: Punjab wheat, good for irrigated conditions
- GW 322: Gujarat wheat, drought tolerant
- NW 1014: Suitable for late sowing

### Sowing Time
- North India (Punjab, Haryana, UP): October 25 - November 25
- Central India (MP, Rajasthan): November 1 - December 15
- South India: November 15 - December 31

### Seed Rate
- Irrigated: 100 kg/hectare
- Rainfed: 125 kg/hectare
- Late sowing: 125-150 kg/hectare

### Fertilizer Recommendation
- Irrigated: N:P:K = 120:60:40 kg/hectare
- Rainfed: N:P:K = 80:40:20 kg/hectare
- Apply full P and K at sowing, split N in 3 doses

### Irrigation Schedule
1. Crown root initiation: 20-25 DAS
2. Tillering: 40-45 DAS
3. Jointing: 60-65 DAS
4. Flowering: 80-85 DAS
5. Grain filling: 100-105 DAS
6. Dough stage: 115-120 DAS

### Common Diseases
- Yellow rust (Puccinia striiformis): Apply Propiconazole 25 EC @ 0.1%
- Brown rust: Apply Mancozeb 75 WP @ 2.5 g/L
- Loose smut: Seed treatment with Carboxin 37.5% + Thiram 37.5%

## RICE CULTIVATION GUIDE

### Varieties
- Swarna (MTU 7029): Most popular, high yield, suitable for flood-prone areas
- IR 64: Short duration, good quality
- Pusa Basmati 1121: Premium basmati, export quality
- Sahbhagi Dhan: Drought tolerant

### Nursery Management
- Seed rate: 20-25 kg/hectare
- Nursery area: 1/10th of main field
- Sowing time: 15-30 days before transplanting

### Transplanting
- Age of seedlings: 20-25 days (short duration), 25-30 days (medium duration)
- Spacing: 20x15 cm or 20x20 cm
- 2-3 seedlings per hill

### Fertilizer
- N:P:K = 120:60:60 kg/hectare
- Basal: Full P, K + 1/3 N
- Top dress: 1/3 N at tillering, 1/3 N at panicle initiation

### Water Management
- Maintain 2-5 cm standing water during vegetative stage
- Drain field 10 days before harvest

### Common Pests
- Brown Plant Hopper (BPH): Apply Imidacloprid 17.8 SL @ 0.5 ml/L
- Stem Borer: Apply Chlorpyrifos 20 EC @ 2.5 ml/L
- Leaf Folder: Apply Monocrotophos 36 SL @ 1.5 ml/L

## COTTON CULTIVATION GUIDE

### Varieties
- Bt Cotton hybrids: Bollgard II (most common)
- Non-Bt: Suraj, Jayadhar (for organic farming)

### Sowing
- Kharif season: May-June
- Spacing: 90x60 cm (irrigated), 60x30 cm (rainfed)
- Seed rate: 2.5 kg/hectare (hybrid)

### Fertilizer
- N:P:K = 150:75:75 kg/hectare
- Apply in 3 splits

### Irrigation
- Critical stages: Squaring, flowering, boll development
- Avoid waterlogging

### Pest Management
- Bollworm: Bt cotton provides resistance; spray Spinosad for heavy infestation
- Whitefly: Apply Thiamethoxam 25 WG @ 0.3 g/L
- Aphids: Apply Dimethoate 30 EC @ 2 ml/L

## SOYBEAN CULTIVATION

### Varieties
- JS 335: Most popular in MP
- MACS 450: Maharashtra
- NRC 37: High protein content

### Sowing
- Season: June-July (Kharif)
- Seed rate: 70-80 kg/hectare
- Spacing: 45x5 cm

### Fertilizer
- N:P:K = 30:60:40 kg/hectare
- Rhizobium inoculation reduces N requirement

### Diseases
- Yellow mosaic virus: Use resistant varieties, control whitefly vector
- Bacterial pustule: Apply Copper oxychloride 50 WP @ 3 g/L

## SOIL HEALTH MANAGEMENT

### pH Management
- Acidic soil (pH < 6): Apply lime @ 2-4 tonnes/hectare
- Alkaline soil (pH > 8): Apply gypsum @ 5-10 tonnes/hectare
- Optimal pH: 6.5-7.5 for most crops

### Organic Matter
- Apply FYM (Farm Yard Manure) @ 10-15 tonnes/hectare
- Green manuring: Dhaincha, Sunhemp
- Vermicompost: 2-3 tonnes/hectare

### Micronutrient Deficiency
- Zinc deficiency: Apply ZnSO4 @ 25 kg/hectare
- Iron deficiency: Apply FeSO4 @ 25 kg/hectare (foliar: 0.5%)
- Boron deficiency: Apply Borax @ 10 kg/hectare

## GOVERNMENT SCHEMES FOR FARMERS

### PM-KISAN (Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi)
- Benefit: ₹6,000 per year in 3 installments
- Eligibility: All small and marginal farmers
- Apply at: pmkisan.gov.in

### Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)
- Crop insurance scheme
- Premium: 2% for Kharif, 1.5% for Rabi
- Covers: Natural calamities, pests, diseases

### Kisan Credit Card (KCC)
- Short-term credit for crop cultivation
- Interest rate: 4% per annum (with subsidy)
- Limit: Based on land holding and crop

### Soil Health Card Scheme
- Free soil testing
- Recommendations for fertilizer use
- Apply at nearest KVK or Agriculture Department

### PM Krishi Sinchai Yojana
- Drip and sprinkler irrigation subsidy
- 55% subsidy for small farmers
- 45% subsidy for large farmers

## CROP ROTATION BENEFITS

### Why Rotate Crops?
- Breaks pest and disease cycles
- Improves soil fertility
- Reduces fertilizer requirement
- Increases overall farm income

### Recommended Rotations
- Rice → Wheat → Mung bean
- Cotton → Wheat → Chickpea
- Maize → Wheat → Sunflower
- Soybean → Wheat → Maize
- Sugarcane → Wheat → Potato

## ORGANIC FARMING PRACTICES

### Biofertilizers
- Rhizobium: For legume crops (soybean, chickpea, groundnut)
- Azotobacter: For non-legume crops
- PSB (Phosphate Solubilizing Bacteria): Improves P availability
- VAM (Vesicular Arbuscular Mycorrhiza): Improves nutrient uptake

### Biopesticides
- Trichoderma viride: Soil-borne diseases
- Beauveria bassiana: Insect pests
- Neem-based products: Broad spectrum pest control
- Bt (Bacillus thuringiensis): Caterpillar pests

### Composting
- C:N ratio should be 25-30:1
- Moisture: 50-60%
- Turning: Every 15 days
- Ready in: 60-90 days

## IRRIGATION MANAGEMENT

### Drip Irrigation
- Water saving: 40-60% compared to flood irrigation
- Suitable for: Vegetables, fruits, cotton, sugarcane
- Subsidy available under PM Krishi Sinchai Yojana

### Sprinkler Irrigation
- Water saving: 25-40%
- Suitable for: Wheat, groundnut, vegetables
- Good for undulating terrain

### Critical Irrigation Stages
- Wheat: Crown root, tillering, jointing, flowering, grain filling
- Rice: Transplanting, tillering, panicle initiation, flowering
- Maize: Knee-high, tasseling, silking, grain filling
- Cotton: Squaring, flowering, boll development

## PEST IDENTIFICATION AND MANAGEMENT

### Common Sucking Pests
- Aphids: Small, soft-bodied insects; cause leaf curl; apply Imidacloprid
- Whitefly: White winged insects; transmit viruses; apply Thiamethoxam
- Thrips: Tiny insects; cause silvering of leaves; apply Spinosad
- Mites: Very small; cause bronzing; apply Dicofol or Abamectin

### Common Chewing Pests
- Armyworm: Caterpillars; eat leaves; apply Chlorpyrifos
- Stem borer: Bore into stems; apply Carbofuran granules
- Pod borer (Helicoverpa): Attack pods/bolls; apply Indoxacarb
- Cutworm: Cut seedlings at base; apply Chlorpyrifos soil drench

### Integrated Pest Management (IPM)
1. Use resistant varieties
2. Crop rotation
3. Biological control (natural enemies)
4. Pheromone traps for monitoring
5. Chemical control as last resort
6. Follow label instructions for pesticides

## WEATHER AND CLIMATE ADVISORY

### Kharif Season (June-October)
- Sow after first good monsoon rain
- Ensure proper drainage to avoid waterlogging
- Monitor for fungal diseases in humid conditions

### Rabi Season (October-March)
- Sow after soil moisture is adequate
- Protect from frost in December-January
- Ensure irrigation at critical stages

### Summer Season (March-June)
- Short duration crops: Mung bean, cowpea, watermelon
- Ensure adequate irrigation
- Mulching to conserve moisture

### Climate Change Adaptation
- Use drought-tolerant varieties
- Adopt micro-irrigation
- Crop diversification
- Weather-based crop insurance
